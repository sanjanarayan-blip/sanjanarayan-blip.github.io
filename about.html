About

Sanjana Rayan

I am a chemical engineer, researcher, and founder. I work at the intersection of deep science and commercial execution. I research things, build products around them, and sell them to the people who need them.

In my own words

I grew up thinking science and business were separate worlds. UC Berkeley showed me they don't have to be. I spent my undergrad years doing real research: quantum chemistry at Lawrence Berkeley National Lab, perovskite crystal growth at the Wu Lab, battery cell characterization at Teserac, and cross-functional engineering at Ford. Each role taught me something different about how science actually moves from idea to impact.

Orbitl came from watching that process up close and seeing how much friction gets in the way. Scientists spend too much time on overhead and not enough on discovery. I started building a fix in September 2025, and in under a year ran 200+ customer discovery interviews, built a GTM motion across 12+ universities, and brought in a 4-person team.

Outside of work I collect vinyl records, think too hard about coffee, care about fashion sustainability, and try to visit somewhere new as often as possible. I led Alpha Chi Sigma, Berkeley's professional chemistry fraternity, as President for 100+ members. I believe the best operators are people who are curious about everything and systematic about a few things.
