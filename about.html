About me

Sanjana Rayan

Chemical engineer, researcher, and founder. I love building things that sit at the intersection of hard science and real commercial impact.

A bit about me

I grew up thinking science and business were separate worlds. UC Berkeley changed that. I spent my undergrad doing real research: quantum chemistry at Lawrence Berkeley National Lab, perovskite crystal growth at the Wu Lab, battery cell characterization at Chemix, and cross-functional supplier engineering at Ford. Every role taught me something different about how science moves from idea to actual impact.

Orbitl came from watching that process up close and noticing how much friction slows scientists down. I started building a fix in September 2025, ran 200+ customer discovery interviews, built a GTM motion across 12+ universities, and put together a 4-person team, all while finishing my degree.

I led Alpha Chi Sigma, Berkeley's professional chemistry fraternity, as President for 100+ members. I care a lot about community, especially making science more accessible and welcoming for people who don't always see themselves in it.

When I'm not working I'm probably listening to vinyl, on my bike, playing badminton, or halfway through a crochet project. I also do charcoal art and think way too hard about coffee. I believe the best operators are curious about everything and systematic about a few things.
